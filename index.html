Projects

The most recent project I have worked on was my capstone project. For our project, we worked on a Skid Mounted Substation and Pump Houses designed for Oil Sands mines and other large mining projects which required portable substations and pump houses to pump liquids such as water and slurry. The substations and pump houses needed to be portable in order to be moved as the mine develops and conditions changed. Through this project, we all developed teamwork skills and more experience using software such as AutoCAD and ETAP.

We designed 3 skids: 1 substation and 2 pump houses. One of the requirements of our project was to draw 3 main drawings of each skid: HV Equipment, Cable Trays, and Lighting layouts. Below are some examples of the drawings we created.

This is our first skid, the substation which provides power to the two pump houses. This drawing shows the lighting layout of the skid.

To differentiate the pump houses, the first pump house does not have a VFD like the second one. The drawing depicted above shows the cable tray layout of the pumphouse.

The second pump house contains a VFD which allows the motor speed to be adjusted. This drawing shows the layout of the equipment while showing the appropriate clearances according to the Candian Electrical Code.
